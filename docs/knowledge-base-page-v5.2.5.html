Documentation Standards

What every support article should provide

Sustainable Catalyst documentation should help readers understand what a product does, complete a workflow safely, recognize limitations, and recover when expected behavior is not available.

Clear purpose

Each guide should explain the task, feature, problem, or decision it is designed to address.

Verified steps

Instructions should reflect the current supported release and identify any version-specific differences.

Sample workflows

Feature guides should include practical examples and synthetic sample data where appropriate.

Expected results

Readers should know what successful completion looks like and how to verify the result.

Troubleshooting guidance

Documentation should identify common symptoms, likely causes, recovery steps, and escalation pathways.

Boundaries and limitations

Guides should explain what a feature includes, excludes, simplifies, or cannot currently support.

Clear Platform Boundaries

Public documentation and private support remain separate

Sustainable Catalyst connects public product guidance with controlled private support without placing personal inquiries, confidential documents, or private correspondence into the public Knowledge Base.

Public

Product Support and Feedback Platform

Provides public documentation, known issues, release intelligence, Guided Resolution, feature suggestions, voting, surveys, and product-support analytics.

  • Public documentation records
  • Product and release intelligence
  • Community participation

Private

Contact and Engagement Platform

Manages private inquiries, secure documents, sender communication, internal review, scheduling, proposals, and engagement lifecycle.

  • Private inquiry records
  • Controlled access
  • Consent-based handoffs

Privacy standard: the public Product Support and Feedback Platform does not collect requester identities, confidential correspondence, or private documents. Those remain within the Contact and Engagement Platform.
